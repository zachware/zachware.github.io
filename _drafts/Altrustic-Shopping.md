While I do most of my reading on a Kindle Voyage I find that some books feel better when read on paper.

I find this to be most often the case for deep philosophical books where I'm likely to write copious notes in the margin. And I have this weird knack for remembering the visual layout of passages that resonate with me, so much so that when I want to recall a specific part of the book it's easier for me to remember what the page looked like than it would be to search a keyword in my Kindle clippings.

Like most people I buy my books on Amazon. New books are cheaper than in bookstores and the books always arrive in 1-2 days.

So this week I decided to buy two particularly strong books for my VTF Capital team (Ego is the Enemy and Daily Stoic). I decided to buy them on Tuesday and wanted to be able to give them to the team on Thurs morning before the team left. My timeline was very aspirational and mildly unrealistic.

Amazon couldn't deliver iI found one copy of each at a Barnes and Noble in LA.
