Thank you for taking the time to reach out and connect. You took the time to write me a personal email and I'm sending an autoresponder back. That's screwed up on a lot of levels.

Unfortunately, I won't be able to reply directly. One of my biggest weaknesses in past years was that I left myself open to giving cognitive space and meeting time to things that didn't align with my primary goals and the goals of my companies.

I said yes too much. I equivocated meeting with people with production. I was wrong.

Since I've said yes to a lot of things already, there's no room left for anything new unless I break one of my past commitments to myself or others. I owe a debt of gratitude to my partners, customers and friends and breaking their trust doesn't help me repay them.

I make <a href="http://zgware.com/now">my current list of priorities public</a>. I have to say no to anything that is not on <a href="http://zgware.com/now">the list of things I'm focusing on now</a> <strong>and</strong> isn't a "hell yeah!"

I wish you all the best in your efforts. And I very much appreciate your support and understanding.
