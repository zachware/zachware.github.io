Every month I send a short email with my favorite books and readings from that month. I’d love for you to <a href="zgware.com/subscribe-here">join that list</a>.

These are the books and works that most shape my world view.

You can pieces of them in <a href="https://www.evernote.com/pub/zachware/readingsgeneral">my massively chaotic Evernote version of a commonplace book</a> (<a href="http://ryanholiday.net/how-and-why-to-keep-a-commonplace-book/">what is a commonplace book</a>) where I keep quotes, marked up articles and book notes. I tag the most potent of that collection as <a href="https://www.evernote.com/pub/zachware/readingsgeneral#st=p&amp;x=bestof&amp;n=bd6d933d-d42d-4275-a68a-e35630e103a2">Best Of</a> and reread them often. Like most piles of papers in my life, I generally know where everything is.

In 2016 my world view, my body and mind shifted more than in any year to date. The key sources of that shift can be found in the <a href="https://www.evernote.com/pub/zachware/readingsgeneral#st=p&amp;n=9829646e-522f-42cf-aebe-2b642a6a2c51&amp;t=2bbde917-47f2-435e-8992-15ef6faab48c">tag called Catastrophe</a>, so named because these works and several similarly timed conversations shattered my life plan and it took me months to recover.

Here’s the short-list.

Ego Is The Enemy

<strong><a href="http://amzn.to/2d25hae">Fooled By Randomness by Nassim Nicholas Taleb</a></strong> 
Jan 2016 - This book kicked off a period of my life I call The Catastrophe. It was the first in a series of readings and experiences that drove me to a deep study of internal vs external orientation. It changed how I few the news, venture capital and just about every other thing. Taleb’s The Black Swan is more popular but FBR shaped my thinking the most.<a href="https://zgware.com/book-notes-fooled-by-randomness/"> Here are my extended notes</a>.

<strong>Paleo Manifesto</strong>
This book isn’t a manifesto about the benefits of the paleo lifestyle but more a pedestrian-friendly introduction to food science. It was the key trigger that led to a massive lifestyle change and obsession with food, micro/macronutrients and ingredient selectivity. It’s the book I most often gift.

<a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/081298160X/ref=as%5C_li%5C_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=081298160X&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=zacwarsperblo-20&amp;linkId=JFTOXDXP4XNLZ5UA">The Power of Habit by Charles Duhigg</a>
*Habits don't change with brute force. By focusing your own or your sales efforts on keystone habits and subconscious patterns, you can affect huge change. Extended notes (https://zgware.com/book-notes-the-power-of-habit-by-charles-duhigg/).

<strong>The Hard Things About Hard Things - Ben Horowitz</strong> (https://www.amazon.com/Hard-Thing-About-Things-Building/dp/0062273205/ref=as_li_ss_tl?sa-no-redirect=1&amp;linkCode=ll1&amp;tag=zgwareblog-20&amp;linkId=ad9a6488090f6010a5d1db53767c8718)
<em>July 2016</em> - This is a must read for any entrepreneur. It’s not so much about secrets than a reminder that Occam’s Razor applies to most of life, including high leverage company building. The simplest, less jargony solution is often the right one. Business books be damned.
