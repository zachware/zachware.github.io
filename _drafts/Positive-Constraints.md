I've recently learned about the concept of positive constraints. I've never been able to articulate what it is I need in order to function effectively. A completely open book with no constraints is difficult for me. I will waste away massive amounts of time and get overwhelmed by selection anxiety.
Positive constraints reflects the idea that with some type of limiter you have less choice and thus can theoretically make easier decisions. It's purely a first world problem.

Less cereal choices. Or owning a home in Las Vegas. Or having a dog. These things tie you down. Without them you are a wanderer. You can do anything. And thus constantly worrying that you didn't make the best decision you could have. No constraints causes me selection anxiety.

I think what has helped me tremendously in this process to buy a home is the financial and mental burden it puts on me. There is no longer a nagging inside feeling that I could be somewhere else. I am here. And will be here for some time.&nbsp;
The best life choices are the ones that decrease optionality.

Paradox of choice. With too many options one will fret over decision. And once one is made we will fret over it wondering if we made the right choice from the choices we had. This is documented extensively in The Paradox of Choice (watch the TED talk). It’s not that we don’t approve of our decision, but with expanded choice comes a greater likelihood we will question what if.

Bought a house. Live a low overhead life.
