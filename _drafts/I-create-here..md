I am very good at organizing work. Some call it playing business. Those things help make work more efficient when there is work being done. The danger is falling into the trap of playing business without actually doing business.

You get the feeling you've accomplished something and in the short-term, you are satisfied. But you haven't moved closer to your goals.

I've had a blog for more than a decade. It's taken various forms. The database behind my blog has been with me since 2007. My blog has taken dozens of forms. I've had editorial calendars, checklists, huge goals. The one thing missing was writing.

I created the perfect canvas for system optimization. I did not, however, populate the system. When inspired to write a great piece I would outline it. The outline would grow and before I knew it the piece had morphed into a dissertation. It's very easy to resist writing a dissertation. And it's very easy to change light bulbs in a theater instead in selling the seats.

I find writing therapeutic. I'm very cerebral. I think about things constantly.

Writing scratches a narcessistic cognitive frustration I have with not getting ideas out of my head.

I need to write.

So here I've changed the canvas. I've intentionally stripped everything down. There is nothing I can customize. This site is hosted on wordpress.com, not my server. I cannot control the code. I cannot add slick marketing features, I can't even add analytics unless I upgrade, which I'm not letting myself do. I'm using the built-in Twenty Sixteen theme.

That's over. Here I've created the canvas to write.

I write multiple times a day, straight into the WordPress editor. Towards the end of the day I publish whichever of them I like the most.

I write first drafts in one sitting, taking no more than 10 minutes. I come back to them as the day passes, delete some, edit some and pick one to publish. Then I move on to other things. This means some of this is raw and not properly edited. Doing this is how I get better.

My old writing is <a href="http://zgw.wpengine.com/writing">here</a>. It will stay there but this is where I write now.

You can get updates <a href="http://eepurl.com/bQvjAj">by email</a> if you like. The email list is also new since I've never been consistent with my old list (which grew quite large) and want to build a new list from zero.

Now go create.
