Recently I've realized that I've spent the last year in near social isolation. One could say I was finding myself. It's been profitable for my physical and mental health. It wasn't an intentional process.&nbsp;

I've been emerging recently &nbsp;

I find this very interesting. I've often wondered how people in highly-visible positions can't see that so many (not all) people surround them because of what they are more so than because of who they are. In my own tiny, insignificant life, I've experienced it. And only once the majority of those relationships vanished did I realize that we're generally incapable of seeing that reality when we're in the midst of it.

You the must actively cultivate authentic relationships. The tide&nbsp;pushes us&nbsp;towards the momentum. It's&nbsp;natural to follow it. But we have to swim against it. And that's incredibly uncomfortable.
